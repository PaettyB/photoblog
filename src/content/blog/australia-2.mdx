## March

Heyhey, welcome back! Next up: my family comes to visit me in Australia! After not being sure if they will arrive at all due to the war in the middle east, they actually showed up! It had only been six weeks since I said goodbye to them at the airport, but I was still very happy to see them again and to show them around, as well as explore new places with them.

We then went to Sydney together, before my parents and my sister went on to do a road trip down the coast all the way to Adelaide, with my sister already departing from Melbourne. On arrival in Sydney, my family  unsuccessfully tried to sort out their flights back home at the airport (the airline refused to give them different flights to avoid the middle east), which means they had to rebook flights for way more money. Setting that aside, we had a great time in Sydney. We booked a hop-on-hop-off bus tour, which took us all around the city to see the main sights. We saw the Opera House, the Harbour Bridge, visited Bondi Beach, took a stroll through the fancy shops in Queen Victoria Building, and went to the top of the Sydney Tower Eye to get a great view of the city.

When my family left to do their road trip, I had the rest of the day to myself. Unfortunately, due to a storm, the weather was quite sh***, so I decided to go to a few museums. In the evening, I walked to the airport, only to find out that all flights were cancelled due to the weather conditions. I quickly booked the cheapest hotel I could find (the airline didn't pay for it) and was set to stay in Sydney for one more day. The next day, the weather was nice again, so I did some more sightseeing, and did the coastal walk from Watsons Bay to Dover Heights. In the end, I was really happy to have spent one more day in Sydney.
